Chapter 1

Division of labour is responsible for the greatest improvements in production. Division of labour contributes in three ways

  1. Increases individual dexterity
  2. Reduce task switching
  3. Facilitation of automation

Incredible number of people work in combination to produce even simple products.

Chapter 2 - Of the Principle Which Gives Occasion to the Division of Labour

  • Propensity to trade leads to specialization which leads to division of labour
  • Humans must rely on trade amongst each other
  • These specializations give rise to different types of people. This is very useful for trade.

Chapter 3 - That the Division of Labour is Limited by the Extend of the Market

  • The market is the bottleneck for the division of labour
  • Water navigation offers great market multiplier
    • Large inland territories usually have a small market and thus a lesser division of labour

Chapter 4 - Of the Origin and Use of Money

  • Trade results in the need to find an easily trade-able good. Metal fits this need; eventually the metal is stamped to certify it. This certification essentially becomes the only thing necessary, resulting in a coin or devaluation of the metal. The is currency.
  • Practical Value = the value of an object in use
  • Value of Exchange = the purchasing power of an object.
  • Practical Value and Value of Exchange correlate inversely

Chapter 5, 6 and 7 Investigate the principles of exchangeable value

Chapter 5 - Of the Real and Nominal Price of Commodities, or of their Price in Labour, and Their Price in Money

  • Labour is the ultimate value of all things
  • Corn is linked with labour very closely and it thus holds it's real value relatively stable

Chapter 6 - Of the Component Part of the Price of Commodities

  • Revenue is derived from three possible sources
    1. Wages from labour
    2. Profits from stock
    3. Rent form land
  • Labour of "inspection and direction" i.e. management creates profits proportional to the stock employed. Thus, more stock result in larger profits.

Chapter 7 - Of the Natural and Market Price of Commodities

definitions

natural Price
when price of commodity perfectly covers the rent, wages and profits needed to produce it.
market price
the actual price for which any commodity is sold
effectual demand
demand for commodity at it's natural price
  • Market prices deviate from natural price in response to supply and demand
  • monopoly, legislation, trade secrets can all effect supply and demand and thus the market price
  • changes in market prices fall mainly on wages and profit. Not as much on rent.

Chapter 8, 9, 10 and 11 explain the causes of the variations in natural price caused by variations in rate of wages, profit and rent.

Chapter 8 - Of the Wages of Labour: Explanation of variation in wages

  • In the battle of unions vs employers, unions are at a disadvantage
  • Increasing natural wealth results in increasing revenue/stock which results in demand for wage labour
    • it is the rate of growth that matters, not the absolute value. Therefor, increase in country population is usually indicative of prosperity
  • Slow growth results in low wages
  • Decline in growth results in starvation
  • Labour wages tend to be more stable than price of provisions. Why? Because increasing cost of provisions results in less demand for labour which decreases wages. However increase in cost of provision also tends to raise wages directly.

Chapter 9 - Of the Profits of Stock: Explanation of variation in rate of profit

The rise and fall in the profits of stock depend upon the same causes with the rise and fall in the wages of labour, the increasing or declining state of the wealth of the society; but those causes affect the one and the other very differently

wherever a great deal can be made by the use of money, a great deal will commonly be given for the use of it; and that, wherever little can be made by it, less will commonly be given for it.

the diminution of profit is the natural effect of its prosperity

  • Increasing stock means decreasing profits which means lower interest rates and increasing wages.
